Melopita: The Soul of Greek Sweets

This traditional Greek honey cake, often called Melopita, is a masterclass in rustic elegance. Unlike dense, spiced Northern European honey cakes, the Greek version is often celebrated for its syrupy, moist crumb and bright Mediterranean flavors.

It typically features a base of semolina or almond flour, which gives the cake a distinct, slightly grainy texture that perfectly captures and holds onto a luscious honey soak.

Key Characteristics:

  • Texture: Incredibly moist and tender, thanks to a generous dousing of honey syrup immediately after baking.
  • Flavor Profile: A fragrant blend of high-quality Thyme honey, zesty orange peel, and warm cinnamon.
  • Toppings: Usually finished with a crunch of toasted walnuts or a simple dusting of ground cinnamon.
  • Variations: Depending on the region (like Sifnos), you might find versions made with fresh myzithra (ricotta-style) cheese, resulting in a texture that sits somewhere between a sponge cake and a cheesecake.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup honey
  • 1 3⁄4 cups white sugar, divided
  • 3⁄4 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 1⁄2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon grated orange zest
  • 1⁄2 ground cinnamon
  • 1⁄4 teaspoon salt
  • 3⁄4 cup unsalted butter
  • 3 eggs
  • 1⁄4 cup milk
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts

Steps

  1. Combine honey, 1 cup sugar, and water in a saucepan; bring to a simmer and cook 5 minutes. Stir in lemon juice; bring to a boil and cook for 2 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool while you prepare the cake.

  2. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease and flour a 9-inch square baking pan.

  3. Combine flour, baking powder, orange zest, cinnamon, and salt in a bowl; set aside.

  4. Beat remaining 3/4 cup sugar and butter together in a large bowl until light and fluffy. Beat in eggs, one at a time. Beat in flour mixture, alternating with milk, beating just until incorporated; stir in walnuts. Pour batter into the prepared pan.

  5. Bake in the preheated oven until a toothpick inserted into center comes out clean, about 40 minutes.

  6. Remove cake from oven and poke holes over the surface with a toothpick. Slowly ladle the cooled honey syrup over cake. For best results, set cake aside for 4 to 6 hours to cool and absorb the syrup before slicing and serving.
